Frequently asked

Seven simple answers.

What is MIRA?

MIRA is a TikTok marketing platform that helps authors create, organize, schedule, publish, and track book content.

Why add Booketeer?

Booketeer is the fastest way to turn your full manuscript into ready-to-pull scenes inside MIRA. Index the book once, then choose scenes instantly instead of searching and pasting them by hand. You can still use MIRA without it by selecting and pasting scenes yourself.

Can I use my own TikTok accounts?

Yes. Connect accounts you own and control. They count toward the connected-account limit included in your plan.

Can I rent TikTok accounts from MIRA?

Yes. Renting is optional. Use accounts you own, rent managed accounts from MIRA, or combine both. Rented accounts are priced separately and do not count toward your plan’s connected-account limit.

Does MIRA post automatically?

Yes. After you approve a campaign, MIRA can publish on your schedule. You can also send posts to drafts instead.

Can I stop a campaign?

Yes. Pause, edit, or stop a campaign at any time.

Does MIRA create content?

MIRA can help create text, visuals, captions, and hashtags, or you can upload finished content of your own.

Prototype notes

One plain promise

The first screen now says exactly what the product does: MIRA helps authors create or upload TikTok content, schedule it across their accounts, and keep the campaign running.

A three-step film

The full-bleed animation is unchanged. Its four scroll states now carry one hero promise plus three plain steps: add content, build the campaign, and press play.

Campaign first, Bucket second

The branded term appears only after visitors understand the result. A Bucket is introduced as MIRA’s automated TikTok campaign—not as a concept they must decode.

Booketeer as the shortcut

Authors can paste scenes manually, or use Booketeer to index the manuscript once and pull ready scenes into MIRA without searching and copying by hand.

The corrected sign-in path

If a new author chooses Sign in, the prototype explicitly says no account exists and no email was sent, then carries the address into account creation. Production should preserve the clarity while evaluating account-enumeration risk.
